TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal is a stunning ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, as a symbol of eternal love. A masterpiece of Mughal architecture blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles, it features intricate pietra dura inlay with semi-precious stones, perfect symmetry, and grand gardens. Construction spanned from 1631 to 1653.

AJANTA & ELLORA

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves are ancient rock-cut cave complexes in Maharashtra, India. These UNESCO World Heritage Sites showcase Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain art from the 2nd century BCE to the 10th century CE. Ajanta is known for murals, while Ellora is famous for the Kailasa Temple.

HAMPI

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage site in Karnataka and the former capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. It is known for Dravidian temples, palaces, and stone monuments like the Virupaksha Temple, Vittala Temple, and Stone Chariot.

RED FORT

The Red Fort (Lal Qila) in Delhi is a 17th-century Mughal palace built from red sandstone. Constructed by Shah Jahan, it includes the Lahore Gate, Diwan-i-Aam, and Rang Mahal. It is where the Prime Minister hoists the national flag on Independence Day.
